Who Are They?

AM (Alana) DeVito is a non-binary, Montreal-based, composer, sound designer, musician, and interdisciplinary artist. Their career as a touring and studio musician spans well over 10 years, multiple countries and numerous instruments, and has now expanded into new forms and new collaborations in digital arts, film scoring, and art installations. In creative A/V installations, compositions and performances, the weaving of ‘virtual kinetic’ soundscapes through vibrant, textural worlds take center stage while unique, sonic architecture project sweeping narratives. Creative computing intertwines with immersive environments while blurring the line between the aural and visual sonic space.

'Creative computing intertwines with immersive environments while blurring the line between the aural and visual sonic space.'

Am DeVito has presented their compositions at the Berlin Off-Kultur Festival, Toronto’s Long Winter Festival, Pop Montreal, Sled Island Music Festival in Calgary, AB, Ypsigrock Festival in Sicily and Vienna’s Freilich Open Arts Series, to name a few. They are currently completing academic studies in Electroacoustics Studies in music and Computation Arts at Concordia University in Montreal.

But there was a time before.

Before returning to school and relocating to Montreal in 2018, Am performed under the moniker, Bobbypin, recording and playing in Berlin and touring around Europe. Before that, they played bass and toured North America and Europe in 2015 with the Montreal band, T0PS.

Am, originally from Burlington, Ontario, moved to Toronto in 2003 to start their first band, Machetes, an Iggy pop-meets-Siousxie Sioux influenced sludge punk band managed by the Cure's keyboardist, Roger O'Donnell.
